# Voice Access campaign storyboard

## Visual language

- Deep ink field, disciplined dot grid, precise route traces, warm gold, moss, clay, and restrained cyan.
- No patient-treatment imagery, synthetic faces, hospital scenes, stock clinicians, glass cards, or decorative dashboards.
- Renata is represented through a purposeful identity marker and her live transcript—not an artificial portrait.
- The interface responds visibly to what is said: correction, consent, location, language, options, interruption, boundary, and handoff.
- Every frame has one primary idea and keeps text inside social-platform safe zones.
- The resident journey is explicitly illustrative. Voice previews request details and hands submission to tap or text. It never claims voice persistence, a provider match, a hub opening, or a verified local result.

## Sequence

### 00:00–00:10.7 — A human opening

Renata’s marker appears beside disconnected path fragments. She asks where to start. Voice Access acknowledges with “Mm-hm,” leaves room, and begins listening. A voice core and responsive waveform establish an active conversation rather than a narrated product demo.

### 00:10.7–00:28.6 — Correction without friction

Renata begins with diabetes, pauses, and corrects herself: she does not want advice; she wants to know where to go. The live transcript revises in place. Voice Access reflects the corrected intent and yields naturally rather than forcing a form field.

### 00:28.6–00:39.3 — Permission and context

The guide requests permission before using county and language. Delaware County and English/Spanish appear only after Renata says yes. The visual system shows consent as a state transition, not fine print.

### 00:39.3–00:51.5 — Request confirmation and interruption

The spoken request becomes a legible preview of the information a non-clinical request would use. Renata interrupts to ask whether a provider has been found. The guide stops; her question takes visual priority.

### 00:51.5–01:05.1 — The boundary

A precise split view distinguishes SozoRock Health’s non-clinical request and preparation role from diagnosis, treatment, prescribing, and provider matching. This is a plain-language response to Renata’s question, not a disclaimer screen.

### 01:05.1–01:11.5 — Resident-controlled submission

Renata asks what the request needs. The interface shows county, language, transportation, and follow-up fields, then hands submission to an explicit tap-or-text confirmation. Voice does not claim to save or submit anything.

### 01:11.5–01:20 — Signature

The route resolves. Voice Access closes: one human conversation, one clearer next step. The approved SozoRock Health lockup and website hold long enough to read.
