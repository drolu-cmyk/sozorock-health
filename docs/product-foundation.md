# Product foundation: from publications to operating platform

## Purpose

SozoRock Health is designed as national access infrastructure, beginning from New York State relationships but not limited to any state, county, or pilot cohort. The platform is not a clinical service and does not replace a provider's professional responsibilities. It enables better pathways into licensed care.

## Rural Equity Blueprint Series, Volume 1: Access Day

*Access Day: Building a Framework for Rural Health Equity in New York State* establishes a practical framework for readiness, health literacy, access equity, workforce alignment, and accountable coordination. Its central product implication is that equitable access cannot depend on a resident having transportation, a personal device, high literacy, reliable broadband, or prior knowledge of a complex healthcare system.

### Product translation

| Framework principle | SozoRock Health requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Access is a system responsibility | Library, community, and home-based hubs have first-class workflows, not a marketing afterthought. |
| Readiness and literacy determine usable access | Voice-first and large-button interfaces, language selection, plain-language prompts, and non-clinical readiness support are launch requirements. |
| Coordinated action outperforms episodic outreach | Hub, provider, and county workflows use a shared access-event model with clear ownership and follow-up states. |
| Improvement must be measurable | Aggregate measures track connection requests, completed connections, access barriers, hub utilization, and time-to-connection without exposing people. |

## Rethinking Rural Governance, Volume 1

*Delaware County, New York: From Compliance to Systems Intelligence* frames rural governance as a shift from fragmented, compliance-led reporting toward proactive, accountable systems intelligence.

### Product translation

| Framework principle | CB-CAP requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Move from reactive reporting to foresight | Show trend changes, access-friction signals, and resource-gap indicators, with forecast features governed by explicit controls. |
| Make evidence actionable | Every visualization supports filters, a plain-language interpretation, and a permitted export or downloadable report. |
| Preserve public trust | CB-CAP operates on aggregate, de-identified events with minimum-cell thresholds and role-based access. |
| Enable local ownership without fragmentation | Internal SozoRock Health operators can view national patterns; interested counties receive scoped access only after approval and activation. |

## Launch measurement model

The first dashboard release will support national, state, county, ZIP, hub type, language, access channel, and time-range filters. It will provide at least:

- request-to-connection funnel and conversion trend;
- median time from request to provider connection;
- hub and channel utilization;
- language-access demand;
- provider capacity and connection acceptance signals;
- non-clinical resource-gap indicators;
- exportable, disclosure-controlled tables and report downloads.

No visualization or export may surface an identifiable resident record or a suppressed small population.

## State-readiness model

Each state has an explicit operational status: `discovery`, `provider-verification`, `launch-ready`, `temporarily-paused`, or `not-active`. A resident can always find the platform and register interest. A connection request is routable only when a matching provider is verified, enabled, and licensed for that resident's state.

## AI-native operating model

AI services are modular capabilities, not a separate product. From day one, the application supports speech input, text-to-speech, translation, and simple access-routing assistance. Feature controls allow operators to restrict a capability by state, organization, channel, language, device type, provider, usage budget, or incident condition without disrupting the core access flow.

AI may clarify options and facilitate access. It must not diagnose, prescribe, determine clinical urgency, or make clinical decisions.
