# The SozoRock Health story

## The promise

Care should not depend on whether a person has a car, broadband, a private device, confidence with forms, or the right words in the moment.

SozoRock Health makes a different kind of access possible: a resident can begin from a library, a community space, a home-based hub, or their own device—and reach a licensed provider through a pathway built for the state where they live.

This is not another portal asking people to navigate a fragmented system alone. It is access infrastructure: practical, local, voice-enabled when useful, and accountable to what a community can actually use.

## The national story, rooted in place

SozoRock Health is available nationwide because access barriers do not stop at county or state lines. Its work begins from New York State relationships and expands through the same question in every community: what must change for a resident to reach care without unnecessary friction?

Every county can be found. Every resident, provider, library, community organization, and county can begin an interest pathway. Provider connections become available only when licensed providers are verified and operationally ready for a resident's state.

## The intellectual foundation

Oluwabiyi Adeyemo authored *Rural Equity Blueprint Series, Volume 1: Access Day* and *Rethinking Rural Governance, Volume 1: Delaware County, New York—from Compliance to Systems Intelligence*.

Together, these works establish the platform's discipline. The first asks communities to make access practical, coordinated, and measurable. The second asks institutions to replace fragmented, reactive reporting with systems intelligence that can guide action. SozoRock Health turns that work into a living platform: hubs that create access and CB-CAP that helps communities understand where action is needed.

## Resident-facing copy principles

- Speak plainly. One action per screen.
- Never imply diagnosis, triage, clinical advice, or guaranteed care.
- State what happens next and who can help.
- Let residents choose voice, text, language, and device path without penalty.
- Use language such as “Find a care pathway,” “Connect with a licensed provider,” and “Get support at a hub.”

## Provider-facing promise

Keep your clinical platform. Expand your pathway to residents who face access barriers.

SozoRock Health helps approved providers receive qualified connection requests only in states where their licensure and availability are verified. The platform does not replace clinical judgment, clinical documentation, or provider responsibilities.

## County-facing promise

Move from isolated numbers to usable access intelligence.

CB-CAP presents protected, aggregated patterns that help authorized operators understand access friction, demand signals, language needs, hub use, and resource gaps. It does not expose residents. It does not turn people into a dataset. It makes the conditions around access visible enough to act on.
